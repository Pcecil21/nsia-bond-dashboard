**NOTE — Important Context:** The sub-1.0x DSCR requires careful interpretation. NSIA's operating budget as presented to the board excludes approximately **$916K/year in off-P&L cash outflows** that flow through restricted UMB accounts (bond principal, land lease loan paybacks, and property taxes). When debt service is paid from the Revenue Fund waterfall (as structured in the Loan Agreement), the cash flow picture is more nuanced than a simple NOI/debt service ratio suggests.

Additionally, FY2026 is tracking significantly better than FY2025:
- YTD Net Income: **$327,097** vs. ($285,765) prior year — a **$612,862 improvement**
- January 2026 revenue up **40.6%** YoY ($220,826 vs. $157,067)
- Total expenses down **21.9%** YoY (primarily due to $596K less in capital expenditures vs. FY2025 chiller project)

**The board should monitor this metric closely through the spring low-revenue months (May-June).**

1. **Building Maintenance Overrun Review** — YTD building maintenance is $37,084 over budget (374%). Management should present a breakdown of costs and whether any qualify as R&R Fund expenditures rather than operating expenses.

2. **Spring Cash Flow Monitoring** — Cash forecast shows negative net cash flow projected for March (-$42K), May (-$90K), and June (-$27K). Ensure sufficient operating cash reserves through the off-season.

### Near-Term (Next 90 Days)

3. **DSRF CD Maturities** — Two CDs totaling $400,000 mature in December 2026 (Mountain Commerce Bank and State Bank of Toledo). Begin discussions with UMB trustee Gena Mayer about reinvestment strategy and rate environment.

4. **Restaurant/Bar Lease Revenue** — Tracking at 50% of budget ($7,000 actual vs. $14,000 budget YTD). Board should address whether lease terms need renegotiation or if there is a collection issue.
